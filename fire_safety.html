Fire Risk Assessments (FRA)

Ultimate responsibility and liability for the adequacy of the fire risk assessment and the implementation of its findings, rests with the person or persons responsible for premises management (“Duty Holder” – Scotland, “Responsible Person” – England & Wales), therefore it is crucial that your organization takes this responsibility seriously and have the systems in place to continually monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of your Fire Risk Assessment.

If your organization’s Fire Risk Assessment is found to be insufficient or ineffective according to the current requirements of the premises the person responsible can face an unlimited fine or up to 2 years in prison* (https://www.gov.uk/workplace-fire-safety-your-responsibilities/enforcement-appeals-and-penalties).

The Fire (Scotland) Act 2005 and the Fire Safety (Scotland) Regulations 2006 replace previous fire safety legislation. The legislation places responsibilities on employers and any other person(s) with control of premises (duty holders) to assess the risk of harm from fire and to put in place appropriate fire safety measures.

All businesses with five employees or more must produce a documented FRA to establish that all the correct measures have been taken regarding fire prevention and detection. The Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 and the Fire (Scotland) Act 2005, emphasizes the importance of preventing fires and reducing risk. It is the responsibility of businesses to ensure the safety of employees and visitors alike. The designated responsible person must conduct, or employ a suitably qualified person to conduct, a fire risk assessment and implement and maintain a Fire Safety Plan.

It is essential that the Fire Risk Assessment for your building is performed by a qualified person. If you are not qualified or are unsure regarding your ability to carry out your own FRA effectively and to the required standard by law, Harris Fire & Electrical Ltd have the qualifications and experience to do so.

Fire Risk Assessment types carried out:

Type 1 – Common parts only (non-destructive)

Type 2 – Common parts only (destructive)

Type 3 – Common parts and flats (non-destructive)

Type 4 – Common parts and flats (destructive)

Fire Alarm Systems

The benefits of having an effective working Fire Alarm System for both commercial (BS 5839 part 1) &/or domestic (BS 5839 part 6) include:

  • Saving lives – the earlier the warning, the sooner your staff and visitors can reach safety.
  • Proactively protecting your business and assets – ensuring minimal disruption to your core business and staff’s ability to continue to work.
  • Proactively protecting property – minimal damage inflicted to building infrastructure and quicker repair timeframes if the fire is caught early.


An early warning is critical to minimizing the threat to human life & damage to building(s) in the event of a fire.
